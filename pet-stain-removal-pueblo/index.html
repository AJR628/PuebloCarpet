Pet Stain & Odor Removal in Pueblo

Specialized enzyme treatments for pet urine, vomit, and persistent odors. Pet-safe, family-friendly, and designed to eliminate the source of the smell rather than mask it with fragrance.

Pets are family, but they leave behind challenges that ordinary cleaning doesn't solve. Pet urine doesn't just sit on the surface. It permeates down into the carpet backing, the padding underneath, and sometimes the subfloor. That's why pet odors return a few days after a regular cleaning: the cleaning addressed what's visible, not what's underneath.

Our approach is built around getting at the source. We use enzyme-based treatments that break down the proteins in urine at the molecular level, so the odor is gone, not just covered up.

Why pet odors come back

When pet urine dries, it crystallizes into salts that hold onto the odor and reactivate whenever they get wet, whether that's a routine carpet cleaning, a humid day, or just a spill. Standard carpet shampoos and store-bought cleaners can mask the smell briefly with strong fragrance, but the salts are still there, and the odor returns.

The fix is enzyme treatment. Enzymes consume the proteins that cause the odor, removing the source rather than covering it. For surface accidents that are caught quickly, this is straightforward. For older or repeated accidents that have soaked through to the padding or subfloor, we apply more concentrated treatment and, in severe cases, may need to lift the carpet to treat the padding directly.

What we handle

Pet urine

Fresh accidents, old set-in stains, and ammonia-style odors that haven't responded to regular cleaning. Surface and deep treatments depending on severity.

Vomit and hairballs

Stomach-acid stains that can discolor carpet fibers if left untreated. Hairball residue and the oils that come with it.

Housetraining accidents

The repeated small accidents that come with puppies and kittens, often in the same few spots that need targeted treatment.

General pet dander

The fine dander, hair, and oils that build up in carpet fibers over time, even in well-trained households. Often noticed most by visitors.

When to call us versus DIY

Small, fresh accidents are usually fine to handle with a clean cloth, cold water, and a basic enzyme spray from the pet store. Where DIY runs out of room is when the stain is set in, when the smell persists after cleaning, when there are multiple accidents in the same area, or when the original accident soaked through to the padding. At that point, professional-grade enzyme treatment and extraction equipment is the realistic path to actually fixing it.

If you're not sure which category your situation falls into, just describe it when you reach out. We'll give you a straight answer about whether it's worth bringing us in or whether you can probably handle it yourself.

Pet- and family-safe by design

Every product we use on pet stain jobs is chosen with pets and family in mind. Treatments are formulated to be safe for pets and kids once the carpet has fully dried, which is typically within 4 to 6 hours. Fragrance-free and eco-friendly options are available on request: just let us know when you book.

What it actually looks like

Every job starts with us looking at the affected areas, including a quick UV-light check if needed to find spots you might not have noticed. We pre-treat each spot with the appropriate enzyme solution and let it dwell while we set up. From there we do a full hot-water extraction across the affected area, which removes the broken-down proteins along with the rest of the dirt and residue.

For severe cases or older stains, we'll talk through what's realistic before starting. In most cases, even longstanding pet odor can be fully eliminated rather than just covered up. Where it can't, we'll tell you that honestly rather than promise something we can't deliver.
